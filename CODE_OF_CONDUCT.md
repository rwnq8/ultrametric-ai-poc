# QNFO Code of Conduct

**QNFO's ultimate aim is to advance scientific understanding and catalyze positive,
systemic global change for the collective benefit of all.**

This Code of Conduct defines the values and standards that govern all QNFO spaces —
repositories, discussions, publications, collaborations, and communications. By
participating in any QNFO space, you agree to uphold these standards. This Code
complements and reinforces the [QNFO Content License Agreement](LICENSE).

---

## 1. Core Values

### 1.1 Collective Benefit
All work within QNFO spaces must serve the **collective benefit of all humanity**.
Research, code, publications, and discussions should advance knowledge and contribute
to solving systemic global challenges. Work that primarily serves private commercial
interests, deepens inequity, or concentrates power is incompatible with this standard.

### 1.2 Open Science
We are committed to **transparency, reproducibility, and shared access**. Research
methods, data, and findings should be openly available whenever possible. Closed,
proprietary, or intentionally obscured work undermines the scientific enterprise and
the mission of collective benefit. Attribution is mandatory — credit those whose work
you build upon.

### 1.3 Ethical Integrity
All contributions and interactions must respect human dignity and the broader
ecosystem. Prohibited uses include — but are not limited to — work that:
- Violates applicable laws or infringes on the rights of others
- Promotes discrimination, violence, or harassment
- Involves exploitative practices or harms ecosystems
- Is broadly considered unethical within scientific and humanitarian communities

No contribution may be used to develop weapons, surveillance systems targeting
vulnerable populations, or technologies designed primarily for coercion or control.

### 1.4 Intellectual Rigor
We value **evidence, logical consistency, and intellectual honesty**. Claims
should be falsifiable and supported by reasoning or data. Speculation is welcome
when clearly labeled as such. Bad-faith argumentation, deliberate misrepresentation,
and fabrication of evidence are prohibited.

### 1.5 Interdisciplinary Respect
QNFO operates at the intersection of physics, information theory, philosophy of
science, artificial intelligence, and computer science. We value insights from all
domains and recognize that transformative ideas often emerge at disciplinary
boundaries. Dismissiveness toward entire fields of inquiry is incompatible with
this standard.

### 1.6 Constructive Collaboration
We assume **good faith** in all interactions. Focus on ideas, not identities.
Critique should be specific, evidence-based, and aimed at improving the work —
not the person. Harassment, personal attacks, and ad hominem argumentation have
no place in QNFO spaces.

## 2. Practical Standards

### 2.1 Attribution
Cite sources. Credit prior art. Acknowledge collaborators. The LICENSE requires
attribution for content use — this Code extends that principle to all interactions.
Plagiarism and uncredited appropriation of others' work are violations.

### 2.2 Non-Commercial Foundation
QNFO is the research identity of **Empowering Change**, a U.S. 501(c)(3) non-profit.
Research is conducted for public good, not private profit. All content is governed
by the QNFO Content License Agreement, which permits non-commercial use only.
Commercial exploitation of QNFO work without a separate agreement is both a license
violation and a breach of this Code.

### 2.3 AI-Accelerated, Human-Guided
We embrace AI as a collaborative tool for knowledge synthesis, hypothesis generation,
and accelerated discovery. However, human judgment remains the final authority.
AI-generated content must be reviewed, validated, and clearly identified as such.
Misrepresenting AI output as human-authored research is prohibited.

### 2.4 Inclusive Language & Accessibility
Strive to communicate in ways that are accessible across disciplines, languages,
and backgrounds. Define specialized terms. Provide translations or summaries where
feasible. Avoid jargon when plain language suffices.

---

## 3. Scope

This Code applies to all QNFO spaces including but not limited to:
- GitHub repositories, issues, pull requests, discussions, and wikis
- Publications, preprints, and datasets
- Official communication channels and social media accounts
- Collaborations and partnerships conducted under the QNFO name
- Conferences, workshops, and presentations representing QNFO work

---

## 4. Enforcement

### 4.1 Reporting
Violations of this Code should be reported to the Principal Investigator at
**rowan.quni@qnfo.org**. Reports will be treated confidentially to the extent
permitted by law.

### 4.2 Consequences
Violations may result in:
- Warning and request for corrective action
- Removal of contributions or content
- Temporary or permanent exclusion from QNFO spaces
- Revocation of license grants under the QNFO Content License Agreement

Actions taken will be proportionate to the severity and pattern of the violation,
with priority given to restorative resolution where feasible.

### 4.3 No Retaliation
Good-faith reports of Code violations are protected. Retaliation against anyone
who reports a concern in good faith is itself a violation of this Code.

---

## 5. Relationship to LICENSE

This Code of Conduct and the QNFO Content License Agreement are complementary.
The LICENSE governs legal use of content. This Code governs conduct within QNFO
spaces. Breach of either may constitute breach of both. In case of conflict between
the letter of this Code and the LICENSE, the LICENSE prevails on legal matters
and this Code prevails on matters of community conduct.
